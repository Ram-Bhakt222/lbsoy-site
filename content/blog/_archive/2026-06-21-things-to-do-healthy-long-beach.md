This is our living guide to healthy, active things to do in Long Beach — updated every few days. The top of the page covers what's happening this week; below it, an expanding directory of the best places and habits for a healthier life across Long Beach's neighborhoods. Bookmark it and check back.

## This Week in Long Beach

### Yoga on the Bluff — Daily, Year-Round

**When:** Daily, 11 a.m. year-round; a second 6 p.m. session runs weekdays through October
**Where:** Bixby Park Annex, Ocean Boulevard at Junipero Avenue (Alamitos Beach / Bluff Park edge)
**Cost:** Free; donations welcome, never required

Long Beach's best free standing wellness ritual. Rotating instructors lead an open-air, all-levels session overlooking the Pacific. Cancelled only when it's raining or the ground is too wet.

### Long Beach Municipal Band Summer Concert Series — Through July 31

**When:** Tuesday evenings, 6:30 p.m. (June 23 through July 31)
**Where:** Rotating across five parks — Whaley Park North, Bixby Annex Park, Larry Curtis Concert Park at Marine Stadium, Los Cerritos Park, and El Dorado Park West
**Cost:** Free

Pack a picnic, bring a blanket and chairs, and take a post-concert walk through your neighborhood park. A free, all-ages reason to spend warm summer evenings outdoors and active.

### Long Beach Farmers Markets — Weekly Across Neighborhoods

**When:** Multiple markets throughout the week: Local Harvest at Bixby Park (Tue 3–8 p.m., Sat 9 a.m.–2 p.m.); Cal State Long Beach Farmers Market (Tue & Wed, 10 a.m.–2 p.m.); Harbor Area markets (Thu, Fri, Sun)
**Where:** Bixby Park (Alamitos Beach), Marine Stadium (Tue & Wed evenings), CSULB (Bellflower Blvd), and multiple other neighborhoods
**Cost:** Free to browse; vendors accept cash and card

The easiest way to eat more local, seasonal produce. Markets rotate by neighborhood and time, so there's almost always one within reach. Walk off your shopping and you've got a complete wellness outing.

### Early Summer Group Fitness & Walk-Up Classes

**When:** Ongoing throughout June and July at studios across Long Beach
**Where:** Long Beach yoga studios (Yoga108, Purple Yoga, Yogalution, JA Yoga, Ra Yoga, and others)
**Cost:** Drop-in rates typically $12–$18; many studios offer free intro classes

Summer is peak season for group fitness. Long Beach studios are packed with outdoor yoga, trail running groups, beach volleyball, and organized walks. Check your local studio or running club's schedule — most post weekly calendars on their websites.

> Events shift week to week — always confirm the date, time, and venue on the organizer's page before you go.

## Best Free & Outdoor Wellness in Long Beach

You don't need a membership to live well here. Beyond the daily free Yoga on the Bluff, Long Beach's coastline and parks are a no-cost gym: the bluff-top paths above Alamitos Beach for walking and breath work, the open lawns of Bluff Park for mobility and stretching, and the beach itself for barefoot walking and swimming. Add the free summer Municipal Band concerts (an easy excuse for an evening walk to the park) and a farmers-market produce run, and you have a genuinely healthy Long Beach day for the price of a coffee donation.

## Best Parks, Bluff & Beach Paths in Long Beach

For cardio without a treadmill, the coast does the work. The **Shoreline Pedestrian Bikepath** runs roughly 4.5 miles of flat, paved, car-free trail from Shoreline Village through to Belmont Shore — split into bike and pedestrian lanes, ideal for walking, jogging, or an easy ride. In **Naples Island**, the **Naples Canals Loop** is a gentle, flat ~2.5-mile walk past the waterfront homes and gondola docks. Up in **Signal Hill**, the Signal Hill Trail and Hilltop Park offer the only real elevation gain inside the Long Beach basin, with a 360-degree view from the top. Pick the one nearest you and make it a standing three-mornings-a-week habit.

## Best Juice Bars, Smoothies & Açaí Bowls in Long Beach

When the marine layer burns off, Long Beach runs on cold-pressed juice and açaí. The standout is **Salud Juice** at 4918 E 2nd St in **Belmont Shore** — one of the city's only USDA-certified-organic, truly raw cold-pressed juice bars (no pasteurization, no HPP), serving since 2012. A few doors down, **Pressed Juicery** (4923 E 2nd St) covers cold-pressed cleanses, açaí bowls, and dairy-free soft serve. On the **Broadway corridor near Bluff Heights**, **Tru Nature Juice Bar** (2225 E Broadway) makes fully vegan açaí and pitaya bowls with no added ice or sugar. Out toward the **Marketplace and PCH**, **Nekter Juice Bar** (6467 E PCH and 2004 Ximeno Ave) and **Tru Bowl Superfood Bar** (6250 E PCH, unlimited toppings) handle the east-side crowd, while **Pur Bowls** (2210 N Bellflower Blvd, in the Trader Joe's plaza) and **Jamba** in **Bixby Knolls** (4085 Atlantic Ave) round out the north and Los Altos sides. Full guide: [Best Juice Bars, Smoothies & Açaí Bowls in Long Beach](/blog/2026-06-16-best-juice-bars-smoothies-acai-long-beach).

## Best Run & Walk Clubs & Group Fitness in Long Beach

For summer running and walking, Long Beach offers strong community groups and competitive events. **Long Beach Run Club** (Meetup-based, ~5 miles per run, with groups meeting at Coronado Ave & Ocean Blvd) is the most established, with Saturday long runs drawing regulars to beach routes. **Run Club Girls** offers paced options including a 2-mile walk pace and 5K jog pace, meeting at 9 a.m. with a low-barrier entry (community-first vibe, no judgment on pace). **Lululemon hosts free run clubs** at several Long Beach locations, particularly popular in **Belmont Shore** and along the PCH corridor, with weekly speed and distance options. For walkers, the **Shoreline Pedestrian Bikepath** (4.5 miles, flat, car-free) and **Naples Island Canals Loop** (2.5 miles, gentle pace) are perfect for group walks — easy to organize a standing Tuesday or Thursday morning group. Larger events include **The Aloha Run** (September 20, Shoreline Aquatic Park — 5K and 10K with family-friendly option) and the **2XU Long Beach Marathon** (October 10–11 — welcomes runners, run clubs, and relay teams). For group training, **The Camp Transformation Center** (Downtown) offers early-morning bootcamps (5 a.m. weekdays), and **Crunch Fitness** (100 Oceangate, Downtown) runs group HIIT and cycling classes year-round. Full guide: [Best Run & Walk Clubs & Group Fitness in Long Beach](/blog/2026-06-18-best-run-walk-clubs-long-beach).
