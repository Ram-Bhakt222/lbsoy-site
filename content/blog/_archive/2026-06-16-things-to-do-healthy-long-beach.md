This is our living guide to healthy, active things to do in Long Beach — updated every few days. The top of the page covers what's happening this week; below it, an expanding directory of the best places and habits for a healthier life across Long Beach's neighborhoods. Bookmark it and check back.

## This Week in Long Beach

### Yoga on the Bluff — Bluff Park, Long Beach

**When:** Daily, 11 a.m. year-round; a second 6 p.m. session runs weekdays through October
**Where:** Bixby Park Annex, Ocean Boulevard at Junipero Avenue (Alamitos Beach / Bluff Park edge)
**Cost:** Free; donations welcome, never required

Long Beach's best free standing wellness ritual. Rotating instructors lead an open-air, all-levels session overlooking the Pacific. Cancelled only when it's raining or the ground is too wet.

### Long Beach Municipal Band Summer Concert Series Kicks Off — Citywide

**When:** Opens Tuesday, June 23; concerts begin 6:30 p.m. (series runs through July 31)
**Where:** Rotating across five parks — Whaley Park North, Bixby Annex Park, Larry Curtis Concert Park at Marine Stadium, Los Cerritos Park, and El Dorado Park West
**Cost:** Free

Pack a picnic, bring a blanket and chairs, and walk it off after dinner. A free, all-ages reason to spend a warm evening outdoors in your own neighborhood park.

### Long Beach Farmers Markets — Multiple Neighborhoods

**When:** Several days a week — Marina (Sun, Alamitos Bay), Bixby Park (Tue & Sat, Alamitos Beach), Bixby Knolls (Thu, Atlantic & 46th), Downtown Long Beach (Fri)
**Where:** Across the city — see the City of Long Beach / LB Fresh listings for current days and hours
**Cost:** Free to browse

The easiest way to eat more local produce. Markets rotate by neighborhood through the week, so there's almost always one within reach.

### International Day of Yoga — Sunday, June 21

**When:** Sunday, June 21 (the daily 11 a.m. Yoga on the Bluff session runs as usual)
**Where:** Long Beach studios and Bluff Park
**Cost:** Varies; the Bluff session stays free

The global day of yoga lands on a Sunday this year. Many Long Beach studios mark it — check your local studio's schedule for any specials, or just show up to the free bluff class.

## Best Free & Outdoor Wellness in Long Beach

You don't need a membership to live well here. Beyond the daily free Yoga on the Bluff, Long Beach's coastline and parks are a no-cost gym: the bluff-top paths above Alamitos Beach for walking and breath work, the open lawns of Bluff Park for mobility and stretching, and the beach itself for barefoot walking and swimming. Add the free summer Municipal Band concerts (an easy excuse for an evening walk to the park) and a farmers-market produce run, and you have a genuinely healthy Long Beach day for the price of a coffee donation.

## Best Parks, Bluff & Beach Paths in Long Beach

For cardio without a treadmill, the coast does the work. The **Shoreline Pedestrian Bikepath** runs roughly 4.5 miles of flat, paved, car-free trail from Shoreline Village through to Belmont Shore — split into bike and pedestrian lanes, ideal for walking, jogging, or an easy ride. In **Naples Island**, the **Naples Canals Loop** is a gentle, flat ~2.5-mile walk past the waterfront homes and gondola docks. Up in **Signal Hill**, the Signal Hill Trail and Hilltop Park offer the only real elevation gain inside the Long Beach basin, with a 360-degree view from the top. Pick the one nearest you and make it a standing three-mornings-a-week habit.

## Best Juice Bars, Smoothies & Açaí Bowls in Long Beach

When the marine layer burns off, Long Beach runs on cold-pressed juice and açaí. The standout is **Salud Juice** at 4918 E 2nd St in **Belmont Shore** — one of the city's only USDA-certified-organic, truly raw cold-pressed juice bars (no pasteurization, no HPP), serving since 2012. A few doors down, **Pressed Juicery** (4923 E 2nd St) covers cold-pressed cleanses, açaí bowls, and dairy-free soft serve. On the **Broadway corridor near Bluff Heights**, **Tru Nature Juice Bar** (2225 E Broadway) makes fully vegan açaí and pitaya bowls with no added ice or sugar. Out toward the **Marketplace and PCH**, **Nekter Juice Bar** (6467 E PCH and 2004 Ximeno Ave) and **Tru Bowl Superfood Bar** (6250 E PCH, unlimited toppings) handle the east-side crowd, while **Pur Bowls** (2210 N Bellflower Blvd, in the Trader Joe's plaza) and **Jamba** in **Bixby Knolls** (4085 Atlantic Ave) round out the north and Los Altos sides.
