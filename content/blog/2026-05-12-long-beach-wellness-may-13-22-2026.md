## This Week in Long Beach Wellness

This is one of the busiest movement weeks Long Beach gets all year. Pride takes over Marina Green on May 16–17 — including a Healing Arts Village & Sanctuary with yoga, meditation, and sound healing — Belmont Shore hosts two separate Sunday road races on the same morning, a free new-moon beach yoga session lands Saturday night, and Yoga on the Bluff runs its full seasonal schedule.

A note on rhythm: the weekend in Downtown Long Beach and Alamitos Beach will be loud, hot, and crowd-dense. Plan one anchor event and protect a recovery window the next morning. The point of a wellness week is regulation, not box-checking.

## Featured Events in Long Beach

### Long Beach Pride 2026 — Downtown Long Beach & Marina Green

**When:** Festival Saturday, May 16, 12 p.m.–10 p.m. and Sunday, May 17, 12 p.m.–10 p.m. Parade Sunday, May 17, 10 a.m.
**Where:** Festival at Marina Green Park, 386 E. Shoreline Dr., Downtown Long Beach. Parade kicks off at Ocean Blvd. & Lindero Ave. and travels along Ocean Blvd. and Alamitos Ave.
**Cost:** Festival ticketed (see longbeachpride.com); parade free to spectate.

Long Beach Pride returns under the theme "Fearless and Free" with three stages, drag, DJs, and more than 150 vendors. For the wellness-minded, the relevant zone is the Healing Arts Village & Sanctuary — yoga, meditation, and sound healing programmed inside the larger event. Pace yourself: hydrate, sit in shade between sets, and treat Monday morning as a real recovery day, not a "back to normal" day.

### Long Beach Pride Run 2026 — Shoreline / Downtown Long Beach

**When:** Sunday, May 17, 8:00 a.m. start
**Where:** Shoreline area, Downtown Long Beach (see registration for exact start)
**Cost:** Paid registration; net proceeds donated to local charities by Shoreline Frontrunners of Long Beach.

The Long Beach Pride Run is the club's major fundraiser and includes a 5K Run/Walk, a 10K Run, and the 200-foot Pride sKIDaddle for kids. 5K walkers and 10K runners must finish within 90 minutes of the 8:00 a.m. gun. The race lands two hours before the parade rolls — possible to do both, but only if you've trained for the distance and have a ride plan back to a quiet space afterward.

### Beach Dash 5K / 10K / 15K / Half Marathon — Belmont Shore

**When:** Sunday, May 17 (same morning as Pride Run)
**Where:** 5400 E. Ocean Blvd., Belmont Shore, Long Beach
**Cost:** Paid registration through A Better World Running.

A flat, beachfront course running out of Belmont Shore — the same 5400 E. Ocean Blvd. staging area used by other Califlow-style Long Beach races. Two organized road events in Long Beach on the same morning is unusual; pick one. The Pride Run is the community fundraiser with a charitable tail; the Beach Dash is the longer-distance option if you're building toward a 15K or half marathon and want a measured race effort on flat coastline.

### New Moon Beach Yoga & Meditation — Long Beach Coast

**When:** Saturday, May 16, 6:30 p.m.–8:15 p.m.
**Where:** Long Beach coastline (specific meet-up published on the Eventbrite listing; verify before heading out)
**Cost:** Free RSVP via Eventbrite.

Outdoor, all-levels beach yoga paired with a guided new-moon meditation to reset intentions before the next lunar cycle. The new moon falls on May 16, 2026, so the timing is real, not arbitrary. If you're someone who tends to over-schedule and under-rest, this is the kind of low-cost, low-pressure event that's easy to actually show up to — bring a mat or large towel, a light layer, and water.

### Yoga on the Bluff — Bluff Park, Long Beach

**When:** Daily at 11 a.m., year-round. Evening classes 6 p.m. Monday–Friday, April through October (in season now).
**Where:** Bluff Park, Ocean Blvd. between Loma and Cherry, just above Alamitos Beach
**Cost:** Donation-based; no one turned away.

Yogalution Movement runs the Bluff sessions seven days a week on the grass overlooking the Pacific. With Pride and two road races landing on the same weekend, a Tuesday or Wednesday 11 a.m. session is a useful counterweight to the noise — same neighborhood, fraction of the cortisol load. The only cancellation rule is rain or wet ground.

### Long Beach Farmers Markets — Multiple Neighborhoods

Five recurring markets in the May 13–22 window, all real food, all weekly:

- **Long Beach Marina, Alamitos Bay** — Sundays, 9 a.m.–2 p.m., 2nd & Marina Drive
- **Bixby Park, Alamitos Beach** — Tuesdays, 3 p.m.–7 p.m. (summer), 130 Cherry Ave.
- **Bixby Knolls** — Thursdays, 3 p.m.–7 p.m. (summer), Atlantic Ave. & E. 46th St.
- **4th Street / Retro Row** — Fridays, 8 a.m.–12 p.m., Cherry & 4th
- **Downtown Long Beach** — Fridays, 10 a.m.–2 p.m., City Place Shopping Center, 3rd St. between Long Beach Blvd. and Pine Ave.

A produce-heavy plate the day before or after a long run, beach yoga, or a packed Pride day is not a wellness cliché — it's the recovery substrate.

## How to Use This Week If You're New to Long Beach Wellness

Start small. Walk to one farmers market. Drop in to one Yoga on the Bluff session at 11 a.m. — donation-based, so the financial bar is zero. If Pride is your scene, treat the Healing Arts Village as the part of the festival you plan around and let the rest be optional.

For Long Beach residents managing a real health condition — chronic low back pain, anxiety, hypertension, post-cancer recovery, post-partum — the right entry point isn't an outdoor group session. It's a one-on-one intake with a clinically trained yoga therapist who can build a protocol around your physiology, your meds, and your weekly capacity.
