## This Week in Long Beach Wellness

The first full week of May lands warm and bright across Long Beach. Marine layer pulls back by mid-morning, the Bluff Park grass dries early, and the city steps into the part of spring when wellness happens outdoors as much as indoors. The week of May 4–14 carries an unusually full slate: a daily ocean-view yoga class at Bluff Park, a Monday-night meditation sit in Belmont Shore, a 1,200-person YMCA philanthropy conference at the Long Beach Convention Center, and a Mother's Day half marathon along the shoreline. Below is what's worth blocking off if you live, work, or run a team here.

## Featured Events in Long Beach

### Yoga on the Bluff — Bluff Park, Long Beach

**When:** Daily, 11:00 AM (evening 6:00 PM sessions seasonally April–October)
**Where:** Bluff Park, on the grass overlooking the Pacific (between Belmont Shore and Downtown Long Beach)
**Cost:** Donation-based

Yogalution Movement & Wellness has been running this daily ocean-view yoga class on the grass at Bluff Park for years, and it remains one of the most beginner-friendly entry points into a Long Beach movement practice. Bring a mat or a beach towel. Donations support Yogalution's home studio at 244 Redondo Ave on the edge of Belmont Heights.

### Long Beach Meditation: "Come As You Are" Monday Sit — Belmont Shore

**When:** Monday, May 4 and Monday, May 11, evenings (check site for exact start time)
**Where:** Gabrielson Chapel inside Bay Shore Community Congregational Church, Belmont Shore
**Cost:** Donation-based

Long Beach Meditation runs an in-person, drop-in mindfulness sit on Monday evenings — no prior practice required, no registration, no pressure. It's one of the few free, weekly, in-person meditation containers in the city, and the chapel itself is small and quiet enough for newer meditators to settle in without the awkwardness of a large studio room.

### NAYDO 2026: YMCA Philanthropy Conference — Downtown Long Beach

**When:** May 6–9, 2026
**Where:** Long Beach Convention Center, 300 E. Ocean Blvd, Downtown Long Beach
**Cost:** Conference registration required (paid)

The 45th annual North American YMCA Development Organization conference brings roughly a thousand YMCA leaders to Downtown Long Beach for four days under the theme "Anchored in Giving." Not a public wellness event, but worth flagging for two reasons: hotel and restaurant capacity downtown will be tight midweek, and HR / benefits leaders attending the conference frequently look for local programming partners to extend their YMCA wellness work back to staff. Conference activities run at the Convention Center, with host hotels at the Hyatt Regency, Marriott Downtown, and Westin Long Beach.

### Long Beach Mother's Day Bloom & Zoom 5K, 10K, and Half Marathon — Alamitos Beach

**When:** Sunday, May 10, 2026 — 7:00 AM to 10:30 AM
**Where:** Start/finish at 5400 E. Ocean Blvd, near Alamitos Beach (Califlow Runs)
**Cost:** Paid registration (5K, 10K, half marathon, 1-miler, Kids Dash)

Califlow Runs' Mother's Day event is a gentle on-ramp for runners who want a real Long Beach race without the October Long Beach Marathon scale. Course follows the shoreline, finish line is at Ocean Blvd, and the Bloom Bar at the finish is part of the draw. If you're a postnatal runner or coming back from a long layoff, the 5K and 1-miler are reasonable goals, and the half marathon is well-supported but real — train for it.

### PyCon US 2026 — Downtown Long Beach

**When:** May 13–19, 2026 (kicks off mid-week, within this window)
**Where:** Long Beach Convention Center
**Cost:** Conference registration required

A second large convention drops into Downtown Long Beach on May 13. Same caveat as NAYDO — capacity will tighten — but this one is also relevant for a Long Beach corporate wellness conversation: a thousand-plus engineers sitting through long conference days are exactly the population who benefit from short, targeted yoga therapy sessions for low back pain, neck and shoulder load, and stress-tolerance breathing. If you run a tech employer in Long Beach or LA County, this is the week to think about onsite or virtual programming for your own team.

### Long Beach Farmers Markets — Multiple Neighborhoods

**When and where:**
- **Sundays, 9:00 AM – 2:00 PM** — Marine Stadium / Local Harvest Farmers Market, by the water at Marine Stadium Park
- **Tuesdays, 3:00 PM – 7:00 PM** — Bixby Park Farmers Market, Bixby Park
- **Thursdays, 3:00 PM – 7:00 PM** — Fourth Street Farmers Market, 4th & Cherry, Retro Row
- **Fridays, 10:00 AM – 2:00 PM** — Downtown Long Beach Farmers Market, The Promenade between 3rd and 5th

**Cost:** Free to attend

Four standing farmers markets cover roughly four neighborhoods and four time blocks, which means there is always a short walk to fresh produce somewhere in Long Beach this week. Marine Stadium and Fourth Street are the most walkable options for Belmont Shore, Naples Island, and Bluff Park residents.

## Long Beach Corporate Wellness Tie-In

This week's slate is a useful map for any HR lead or benefits manager running a Long Beach team. The Mother's Day Bloom & Zoom is a low-stakes way to seed a running culture for your office. NAYDO and PyCon will fill downtown hotels with conference attendees who routinely flag back pain, hip stiffness, and sleep disruption from long sitting days — and the same patterns hold for your local staff sitting in Long Beach Convention Center adjacent offices the rest of the year.

If your Long Beach office wants to translate this kind of week into a year-round program, it's worth looking at structured yoga therapy rather than ad-hoc yoga classes. Yoga therapy is delivered by IAYT-credentialed yoga therapists trained to work with real clinical conditions — low back pain, hypertension, anxiety, post-cancer recovery — in ways a standard group class isn't designed for. Long Beach School of Yoga partners with [My Yoga Network's corporate wellness programs](https://myyoganetwork.com/corporate-wellness-programs) to bring yoga therapists onsite or virtually to employer teams in Downtown Long Beach, Bixby Knolls, the airport corridor, and the port. Start with the [LBSOY corporate wellness page](/corporate-wellness) or book a [free consultation](/free-consultation) to scope a program for your team.

For individual staff or family members dealing with a specific clinical condition, our [yoga therapy](/yoga-therapy) page walks through what one-on-one work looks like and how it differs from a community class.

## How to Use This Week If You're New to Long Beach Wellness

If you're new to a wellness practice, the gentlest on-ramp this week is Yoga on the Bluff at 11 AM — outdoors, donation-based, no commitment. Add the Monday "Come As You Are" sit in Belmont Shore for a free meditation introduction. Layer in one farmers market visit and one walk along the Shoreline Pedestrian Bikepath. That single week is enough to know whether a regular Long Beach wellness practice has a place in your life — without spending real money or making a real commitment until you do.

If you're already a runner, the Mother's Day Bloom & Zoom on May 10 is a sensible spring goal. If you're already a meditator or yoga therapy client, this is also a fine week to add one new venue to your rotation — Long Beach is small enough that crossing neighborhoods (Belmont Shore on Monday, Bluff Park on Tuesday, Marine Stadium on Sunday) gives the practice texture without much driving.
