## This Week in Long Beach Wellness

Spring in Long Beach has officially hit its stride. The marine layer burns off by mid-morning, the bluff grass is finally green, and Long Beach's outdoor wellness calendar is quietly one of the best on the California coast. Between Friday, April 25 and Sunday, May 3, 2026, locals in Bluff Park, Belmont Shore, Alamitos Beach and Naples Island have free yoga seven mornings a week, a community beach cleanup at Belmont Pier, two weekend farmers markets, and seasonal sunset classes that only run from April through October.

Here's what we're recommending at Long Beach School of Yoga — verified venues, real schedules, no filler.

## Featured Events in Long Beach

### Daily Free Yoga at Bluff Park, Long Beach (Alamitos Beach)

**When:** Every day, 11:00 AM. Sunset class Monday–Friday at 6:00 PM (seasonal, April–October).
**Where:** Bluff Park, above Junipero Beach, Long Beach, CA. Free or metered street parking; paid lot at 1 Junipero Ave.
**Cost:** Free. Donations accepted but not required.

Yogalution's "Yoga on the Bluff" has quietly been one of Long Beach's most consistent wellness traditions for nineteen years. A daily outdoor yoga class, open to all levels, on the grass overlooking the Pacific. No reservation, no app, no contract — just show up with a mat. If you've been meaning to start (or restart) a practice, this is the lowest-friction entry point in the city. The evening sessions are the sweet spot this week; sunset over the Queen Mary and the San Pedro breakwater is worth the walk alone.

### First-Saturday Beach Cleanup at Belmont Pier, Long Beach

**When:** Saturday, May 2, 2026, 10:00 AM – 12:00 PM
**Where:** Belmont Pier (Ocean Blvd. at 39th Place), Belmont Shore, Long Beach
**Cost:** Free. RSVP and digital waiver via Surfrider Long Beach.

The Surfrider Foundation's Long Beach chapter runs a community beach cleanup on the first Saturday of every month at Belmont Pier. Two hours, gloves and buckets provided, families welcome. For Long Beach residents who want movement that doubles as stewardship, this is the move. It's also an underrated form of corporate team-building — a handful of Long Beach offices now use the May cleanup as a quarterly community day, often paired with an afternoon mindfulness session.

### Sunday Morning at Long Beach Southeast Farmers Market (Alamitos Bay / Naples)

**When:** Every Sunday, 9:00 AM – 2:00 PM
**Where:** Alamitos Bay Marina parking lot, across from 2ND & PCH, Long Beach
**Cost:** Free to browse.

Of the four certified farmers markets in Long Beach, the Sunday Alamitos Bay market is the most wellness-dense. Fresh seafood straight off the boats, local produce, artisanal ferments, small-batch skin care, and enough walking-distance coffee to make a morning of it. Pair it with a post-market walk on the Naples Island canals — flat, shaded, roughly two miles door-to-door, and one of the most meditative neighborhood loops in Southern California.

### Donation-Based Studio Classes in Long Beach

**When:** Daily. Check yogalutionmovement.com for current schedule.
**Where:** Yogalution Movement & Wellness, 244 Redondo Ave, Long Beach (Rose Park / Bluff Heights)
**Cost:** Suggested donation $20, drop-ins welcome.

If the weather turns (April in Long Beach can still surprise you with a Pacific system), Yogalution's indoor studio on Redondo Ave runs a full weekly schedule of traditional yoga, breathwork and meditation — no reservations, no membership pressure. Good contingency if you want to keep the week's routine without getting rained out.

## Planning Your Long Beach Wellness Week

A simple template for the coming nine days:

- **Weekday mornings (Apr 27 – May 1):** 11 AM Yoga on the Bluff. Thirty minutes of coastal walking after.
- **Weekday evenings (Apr 27 – May 1):** 6 PM sunset class on the Bluff if schedule allows — weather-dependent but worth it.
- **Saturday, May 2:** 10 AM – 12 PM Surfrider beach cleanup at Belmont Pier, then brunch in Belmont Shore.
- **Sunday, May 3:** Alamitos Bay farmers market, Naples Island walking loop, optional 11 AM Bluff class to close the week.

Nothing on that list costs more than parking, and most of it is free. For Long Beach residents trying to rebuild a consistent wellness baseline — particularly those returning from injury, long-haul illness, or a stressful work quarter — stacking outdoor movement, community, and nutrition across a single week is more durable than any single class.
