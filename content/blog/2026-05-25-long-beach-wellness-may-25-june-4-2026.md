## This Week in Long Beach Wellness

Memorial Day weekend in Long Beach is the soft opening of summer. The marine layer is still doing its slow morning burn-off over Bluff Park, the Shoreline Bikepath is starting to feel busy again, and the farmers markets are full of the first stone fruit of the season. Between now and June 4, there's a steady mix of free outdoor movement, neighborhood farmers markets, restorative sound work, and one big convention-center weekend — enough that any Long Beach local can string together a real wellness week without leaving the city.

Here's what's actually happening, with verified venues and times, and a few notes on how to use it if you work in HR, benefits, or operations at a Long Beach employer thinking about a more durable corporate wellness program.

## Featured Events in Long Beach

### Yoga on the Bluff — Bluff Park, Long Beach

**When:** Daily, 11:00 AM (a second sunset class added during spring and summer — check Yogalution Movement's site for that week's evening time)
**Where:** Bluff Park, between Junipero Ave and 36th Place, overlooking the Pacific (Bluff Heights / Alamitos Beach edge)
**Cost:** Free / donation-based

Yogalution Movement runs free, donation-based yoga on the bluff every day of the week as a long-running commitment to the Long Beach community. It's the most reliable outdoor yoga class in the city — bring a mat or a towel, show up a few minutes early, and you'll find a real cross-section of Long Beach (retirees, hospitality workers post-shift, parents with kids, people in office clothes on lunch). During summer they add a second class so you can practice with the sunset behind the breakwater.

### Memorial Day Holy Chair Yoga — Belmont Shore

**When:** Monday, May 25, 4:00 PM
**Where:** Gabrielson Chapel, Bay Shore Community Congregational Church, Belmont Shore
**Cost:** Free / donation

Holy Chair Yoga at Bay Shore Church runs every Monday at 4:00 PM in the Gabrielson Chapel and continues through Memorial Day week. It's a gentle, accessible chair-based class — the right call for anyone who's been dealing with low back pain, post-surgical limitations, balance issues, or simply wants to practice without getting on and off the floor. The chapel is small and quiet, which makes it one of the better Belmont Shore options for older Long Beach residents who'd rather not navigate a packed studio.

### Bixby Park Farmers Market — Alamitos Beach

**When:** Tuesday, May 26, 3:00 PM – 8:00 PM and Saturday, May 30, 9:00 AM – 2:00 PM
**Where:** Bixby Park, 130 Cherry Ave, Long Beach 90803 (Alamitos Beach / east of Downtown Long Beach)
**Cost:** Free to attend

Run by Harbor Area Farmers' Markets, the Bixby Park Certified Farmers' Market is one of the most consistent weekly markets in the city. Tuesday evenings are the better bet if you're coming from work — it's a real after-work crowd, with prepared food, fresh produce, and live music depending on the week. Saturday mornings are the larger market and a good way to stack a workout (walk down from Bluff Park) with a real grocery run.

### Marine Stadium Farmers Market — Belmont Shore / Naples Island

**When:** Wednesday, May 27, 3:00 PM – 8:00 PM
**Where:** Marine Stadium, Appian Way & Nieto, Long Beach (Belmont Shore / Naples Island border)
**Cost:** Free to attend

Wednesday afternoons at Marine Stadium hit a different note from Bixby — quieter, more boat-and-paddle community, less downtown energy. The site itself is one of the most underrated assets in Long Beach: a calm-water rowing course originally built for the 1932 Olympics. Pair the market with a 30-minute walk around the stadium or down to Mother's Beach, and you've replaced a gym session.

### Sound Bath and Restorative Yoga Programming — Zaferia and Marina Pacifica

**When:** Late May through early June (check venue calendars for exact dates this week)
**Where:** Compound (Zaferia) and West Coast Sweat (Marina Pacifica, 6324 E Pacific Coast Hwy)
**Cost:** Varies by event (typically $25–$40)

Two of the more reliable Long Beach sound bath and restorative venues continue their late-May programming. Compound, in the Zaferia district, runs a rotating mix of yoga, breathwork, sound baths, and meditation through its cultural-complex calendar. West Coast Sweat in Marina Pacifica adds a restorative yoga / Reiki / crystal bowl sound bath event for people who want the nervous-system reset without the gym energy. Both are good options if you're navigating burnout, sleep disruption, or anxiety and a power-class isn't what your body needs right now.

### Long Beach Scuba Show — Downtown Long Beach

**When:** Saturday, May 30, 9:30 AM onward
**Where:** Long Beach Convention & Entertainment Center, Downtown Long Beach
**Cost:** See event site for ticketing

Not a wellness event in the strict sense, but worth flagging — the Scuba Show is one of the city's longest-running conventions and brings a noticeable spike in hotel demand and downtown foot traffic. If you live or work near the Convention Center, expect parking to tighten that weekend; if you're a hotel operator or restaurant manager, this is also a soft test run for the June convention slate.

### Looking Just Ahead: Pacific Islander Festival — Aquarium of the Pacific

**When:** Saturday and Sunday, June 6 – 7, 2026
**Where:** Aquarium of the Pacific, 100 Aquarium Way, Downtown Long Beach
**Cost:** Included with general admission

Just outside this week's window but worth marking the calendar — the Aquarium's Pacific Islander Festival returns for its 23rd year on June 6–7, celebrating Chamorro, Fijian, Hawaiian, Māori, Marshallese, Tahitian, Tongan, and Samoan cultures. Free with general admission, family-friendly, and one of the more thoughtful cultural events on the Long Beach summer calendar.

## How to Use This Week If You're New to Long Beach Wellness

If you're new to the city or just rebuilding a routine after a long winter, here's a sensible Memorial Day-through-early-June stack: one Yoga on the Bluff class on Monday or Tuesday morning, one farmers market run (Bixby Park Tuesday or Marine Stadium Wednesday), one restorative or sound bath session midweek at Compound or West Coast Sweat, and one weekend walk along the Shoreline Bikepath from Belmont Shore back toward Downtown Long Beach. That's four anchors in a week, zero gym membership, and every venue inside the city.
