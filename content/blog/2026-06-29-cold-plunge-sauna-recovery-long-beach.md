## Recovery Is Long Beach's Fastest-Growing Wellness Habit

A few summers ago, "recovery" in Long Beach meant a foam roller and a nap. Today it's a category. Private contrast-therapy suites, communal cold plunges, and full-spectrum infrared saunas have opened up and down Pacific Coast Highway and out at the Long Beach Exchange — and locals who run, surf, lift, or just sit at a desk all day are folding heat-and-cold sessions into their week. The pitch is simple: alternating sauna heat with cold-water immersion is said to boost circulation, calm the nervous system, and help you recover faster and sleep deeper. Whether you're a Belmont Shore marathoner or a Bixby Knolls parent stealing 40 quiet minutes, here's where to go in Long Beach.

A quick honest note before the list: the research on cold plunge and sauna is still developing, and the strongest, best-documented benefit of regular sauna use is simply that it feels good and helps people relax and de-stress. Treat the recovery claims below as what the studios and enthusiasts report, not medical promises — and check with your doctor before cold immersion if you have a heart condition.

## Contrast Therapy in Belmont Shore & Belmont Heights, Long Beach

The stretch of PCH through Belmont Shore and Belmont Heights has quietly become Long Beach's recovery row.

**SWTHZ (SweatHouz) Long Beach** — 6480 E Pacific Coast Hwy, Ste 152 (2nd & PCH). The polished newcomer of the bunch: up to 60 minutes in a private luxury suite with an infrared sauna, a cold plunge, and a vitamin-C shower, all to yourself. It's the most "just book it on your phone and go" option in town, and the private-suite format makes it an easy first plunge if communal spaces feel intimidating.

**Pause Studio Long Beach** — 6242 Pacific Coast Hwy (Belmont Heights). Part of a respected Southern California recovery brand, Pause pairs structured contrast therapy — private sauna and cold plunge suites — with IV hydration and other recovery services. Good for the data-minded who want a programmatic, performance-framed session.

**Human Aeon — The Hidden Sanctuary** — 121 Belmont Ave (Belmont Heights). A more grounded, sanctuary-style take on natural contrast therapy, with cold plunge and saunas in a calm, design-forward space just off the busy Belmont Heights corridor. The vibe leans restorative rather than athletic.

## Cold Plunge & Cryotherapy Around the Long Beach Traffic Circle

**ReEnergized** — 4434 E Pacific Coast Hwy (near the Traffic Circle). This is the human-optimization corner of the Long Beach recovery scene. Alongside contrast therapy — infrared sauna paired with either a 39°F cold plunge or cryotherapy — ReEnergized stacks hyperbaric oxygen, red-light therapy, and whole-body and targeted cryotherapy. If you want to go past the basic plunge-and-sweat and experiment with the full modern recovery menu, this is the Long Beach address for it.

## A Communal Plunge: Cellevate in East Long Beach

**Cellevate** — 6445 E Pacific Coast Hwy. The social outlier. Instead of solo suites, Cellevate is built around a large 10-person in-ground cold plunge and a 20-plus-person infrared sauna, with breathwork and movement programming layered on top. It's recovery as community — closer to a wellness club than a clinic, and a genuinely fun way to plunge with friends rather than white-knuckle it alone. Worth checking the class calendar before you go, since much of the experience is guided and scheduled.

## Infrared Sauna at the Long Beach Exchange

**Perspire Sauna Studio — Long Beach Exchange** — 4201 McGowen St (inside the LBX outdoor center, East Long Beach). If your entry point to recovery is heat rather than cold, Perspire is the most accessible option. Each 40-minute private session puts you in a full-spectrum infrared sauna with medical-grade red-light therapy and your own screen (Netflix, Hulu, YouTube), and select suites add a cold "SNO" shower for a contrast finish. Hours run 7 a.m.–9 p.m. on weekdays and 8 a.m.–8 p.m. weekends, so it slots around a work schedule, and the LBX location means you can pair it with errands or a healthy bite nearby.

## How to Actually Use Recovery in Your Long Beach Week

You don't need to do all six. Pick a format that matches your nervous system: if you're wired and need to come down, lead with a longer infrared sauna and a short, gentle cold finish. If you're sluggish and need to switch on, the cold plunge is the lever. A common rhythm is a hot-cold-hot-cold cycle ending on cold, but beginners do fine with one sauna round and a brief 30–60-second plunge. Go in the morning for an energizing reset, or in the evening to wind down — many locals report deeper sleep after a sauna-forward session. And remember the cheapest version of all of this is already here: a brisk swim off Alamitos Beach followed by sun-warmed sand is contrast therapy too.

Recovery also works best alongside the practices that build resilience in the first place. Breath, mobility, and a calmer baseline nervous system are exactly what a structured yoga therapy practice trains — which is why plunge-and-sauna devotees and yoga therapy clients tend to be the same people.
