## Eating Clean Across Long Beach

A healthy day in Long Beach doesn't end at the bluff or the bike path — it carries into where you eat. The city has quietly built one of the deepest healthy-food scenes in Southern California, from raw vegan brunch spots to chef-driven poke and farm-sourced Mediterranean. This guide maps the best healthy restaurants and cafés across Long Beach's neighborhoods, with real addresses so you can find them. Every spot below was verified open in 2026; we don't list places that have closed.

Whether you're refueling after a morning swim or just want a lunch that leaves you sharper instead of sleepy, here's where Long Beach locals go to eat well.

## Belmont Shore & Belmont Heights: Bowls, Brunch & Poke

The east side is the densest cluster of healthy eating in the city, anchored by the 2nd Street corridor.

**The Wild Chive** (2650 E Broadway, Belmont Heights) is the neighborhood's standout for plant-based comfort food. The menu leans into hearty bowls, breakfast plates, and globally inspired sandwiches — think banh mi bowls and shroom patty melts — all fully vegan, served with indoor, outdoor, and dog-friendly patio seating. It's a weekend brunch favorite, open Tuesday through Sunday from 8 a.m. to 3 p.m.

For poke, **Poke Pola** (5040 E 2nd St, Belmont Shore) has been a 2nd Street fixture since 2016, building made-to-order bowls of fresh fish, vegetables, and rice or greens — a genuinely light, protein-forward lunch you can eat before or after a walk along the shore. They're open daily, 11 a.m. to 9 p.m.

Just up the street, **Open Sesame** (5215 2nd St, Belmont Shore) is the local crowd favorite for Lebanese and Mediterranean food, with an unusually deep bench of vegetarian, vegan, and raw options alongside classic fattoush and tabbouleh salads. Mediterranean cuisine consistently ranks among the most heart-healthy ways to eat, and Open Sesame makes it easy to order that way.

## Downtown & East Village: Plant-Based Power Lunch

Long Beach's urban core has become a hub for affordable, fast healthy food.

**Higher Taste, Plant-Based** (340 E 4th St, East Village) is the go-to for açaí bowls, smoothies, and plant-based bowls and burgers in the walkable Arts District. It's a reliable midday stop when you want something nutrient-dense that won't slow you down — the kind of lunch that pairs naturally with an active Long Beach routine.

A few blocks away, **Everytable** (421 W Broadway, Downtown) was built around a simple idea: fresh, prepared, dietitian-designed meals priced to be accessible in every neighborhood. The grab-and-go salads, grain bowls, and proteins make it one of the easiest healthy lunches Downtown, and a second location operates at 3750 Kilroy Airport Way for the business district.

## Bixby Knolls & the Westside: Mediterranean & Vegan Mexican

North and west Long Beach hold their own.

In **Bixby Knolls**, **The Green Olive** (3580 Long Beach Blvd) serves fresh, affordable Mediterranean — large salads, kebab plates with chicken, salmon, or kofta, and lighter pita wraps. It's an easy spot to build a balanced plate, and a welcome healthy anchor for the north-city neighborhood.

Out on the **Westside**, **PlanTita's Kitchen** (1950 W Willow St) proves plant-based food doesn't mean giving up flavor or culture: it's a fully vegan Mexican kitchen that reimagines tacos, burritos, and comfort classics without animal products. It has become one of the most talked-about vegan spots in the city.

## East Long Beach: Superfood Bowls & Plant-Based Classics

Toward the El Dorado side of town, **Blue Bowl Superfoods** (4150 McGowen St) specializes in açaí and superfood bowls layered with fruit, granola, and nut butters — a clean post-workout reward that's become a local staple. And at the Marketplace, **Veggie Grill** (6451 E Pacific Coast Hwy) offers a full menu of plant-based burgers, sandwiches, salads, and bowls in a fast-casual setting that's friendly to the whole family, vegan or not.

## How to Build a Genuinely Healthy Meal Out

Eating out in Long Beach can absolutely support your health goals — the trick is ordering with intention. A few habits that travel well across all the spots above: lead with vegetables and a lean protein, treat grains and tortillas as the side rather than the center, and watch the add-ons (dressings, sauces, and sweetened açaí toppings are where calories quietly stack up). Drink water, and let the meal fuel the rest of an active day rather than anchor you to the couch.

The neighborhoods make it easy. Walk to 2nd Street for poke, bike to the East Village for a bowl, or stroll Bixby Knolls for a Mediterranean plate — the meal becomes part of the movement, not a break from it.
