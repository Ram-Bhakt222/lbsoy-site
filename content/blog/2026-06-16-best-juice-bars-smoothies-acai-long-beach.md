## Why Long Beach Runs on Juice and Açaí in Summer

When the June marine layer finally burns off, the lines outside Long Beach's juice bars get longer. A cold-pressed green juice or a frozen açaí bowl is the easiest healthy habit to add to a beach day — no membership, no commitment, just a portable hit of fruit, greens, and protein. The good news is that Long Beach has more genuinely good options than most cities its size, spread across nearly every neighborhood. This is our running list of where to go, organized so you can find the closest one to wherever you already are.

A quick note on quality, because "juice bar" covers a lot of ground. The biggest difference is how the juice is made and stored. **Cold-pressed** juice is extracted under pressure rather than with a fast spinning blade, which keeps more of the produce intact and avoids heating it. Some bars then pasteurize or use high-pressure processing (HPP) to extend shelf life; a smaller number serve their juice truly raw. None of that makes a chain smoothie "bad" — it just helps you match the spot to what you actually want.

## Belmont Shore: Long Beach's Juice Capital

Second Street in **Belmont Shore** has the densest cluster of juice in Long Beach, and it's anchored by **Salud Juice** at 4918 E 2nd St. Salud has been serving the neighborhood since 2012 and is one of the only USDA-certified-organic juice bars in the city that bottles in glass and serves its juice truly raw — no pasteurization, no HPP. The menu runs from signature cold-press juices and smoothies to immunity shots, açaí bowls, and superfood lattes, and it's open early (7 a.m. most days) for a pre-beach or pre-work stop.

A few doors down at 4923 E 2nd St, **Pressed Juicery** brings the polished chain version of the same idea: cold-pressed juices, juice cleanses, açaí bowls, dairy-free soft serve, wellness shots, and protein smoothies. If Salud is the local original, Pressed is the reliable, consistent option when you want a known quantity. Having both within a block makes Belmont Shore the natural first stop on any Long Beach juice tour — pair it with a walk along the nearby Shoreline Pedestrian Bikepath and you've built a genuinely healthy morning.

## The Broadway Corridor and Bluff Heights

Heading inland from the water, **Tru Nature Juice Bar** at 2225 E Broadway sits on the edge of **Bluff Heights** and **Alamitos Beach**, and it's the pick for anyone eating fully plant-based. Everything is vegan and made without added ice, sugar, or sorbet, so the açaí and pitaya bowls taste like fruit rather than dessert. Beyond bowls, Tru Nature does fresh juices, smoothies, vegan salads, wraps, and protein snacks, which makes it more of a light-lunch destination than a grab-and-go counter. It's an easy detour after a morning at Bluff Park or the daily free Yoga on the Bluff class just down the hill.

## East Long Beach: PCH, the Marketplace and Los Altos

The east side of Long Beach is well covered. **Nekter Juice Bar** has multiple locations here — including 6467 E Pacific Coast Highway near the Marketplace and 2004 Ximeno Avenue — serving handcrafted açaí bowls, smoothies, fresh and cold-pressed juice, and cleanses that you can customize to your diet. Also on PCH, **Tru Bowl Superfood Bar** at 6250 E Pacific Coast Hwy is the spot for an over-the-top açaí bowl: it's known for unlimited toppings, so it's a favorite with families and anyone feeding a post-workout appetite.

Out toward **Los Altos** and the Trader Joe's plaza at 2210 N Bellflower Blvd, **Pur Bowls** has built a following for vegan açaí bowls and dessert-style bowls — a good option if you're already running errands on the north-east side near Cal State Long Beach. These three give the east side enough range that you rarely need to drive to the water for a decent bowl.

## North Long Beach and Bixby Knolls

Up Atlantic Avenue in **Bixby Knolls**, **Jamba** in the Bixby Knolls Plaza at 4085 Atlantic Ave keeps the north side stocked with smoothies, smoothie bowls, and juices. It's a chain, but it's the most convenient healthy quick-stop for that corridor, and it pairs naturally with a Saturday-morning walk or the Thursday Bixby Knolls farmers market. (Worth noting for longtime locals: the former Juice Lab of Bixby Knolls on Atlantic has closed, so Jamba and the nearby markets are your current go-tos in that pocket.)

## How to Build a Healthy Long Beach Day Around It

The trick isn't finding a juice bar — it's stacking it with the free stuff around it. A standing routine that works in almost any Long Beach neighborhood: a morning walk or the free 11 a.m. Yoga on the Bluff class, a cold-pressed juice or açaí bowl afterward, and a farmers-market produce run for the week. Do that two or three mornings a week and the healthy habit builds itself, no gym required.
