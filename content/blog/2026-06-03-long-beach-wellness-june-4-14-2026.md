## This Week in Long Beach Wellness

June in Long Beach means the gloom is still hanging over the shoreline most mornings, which is exactly why locals love it — cool, overcast starts are some of the best conditions of the year for outdoor movement before the afternoon sun breaks through. Between June 4 and June 14, the city's wellness calendar runs on its summer rhythm: free daily yoga over the ocean, two strong weekly farmers markets, early-morning walking groups along the water, and one big cultural festival weekend at the Aquarium.

Here's what's verified and happening, with venues, times, and costs — plus a note at the end for anyone responsible for employee wellbeing at a Long Beach company.

## Featured Events in Long Beach

### Yoga on the Bluff — Alamitos Beach / Bluff Heights

**When:** Daily, 11:00 AM, plus weekday sunset classes at 6:00 PM (running April through October)
**Where:** Bluff Park, near E Ocean Blvd and Junipero Ave
**Cost:** Free / donation-based

Yogalution Movement's daily class on the bluff remains the anchor of free outdoor wellness in Long Beach — no reservation, no membership, just show up with a mat or towel. June is one of the best months for it: the marine layer keeps the 11 AM session cool, and the 6 PM weekday class catches the long pre-solstice evenings over the breakwater. If you've never done a single yoga class in your life, this is the lowest-friction starting point in the city.

### Pacific Islander Festival — Aquarium of the Pacific, Downtown Long Beach

**When:** Saturday–Sunday, June 6–7, during regular Aquarium hours
**Where:** Aquarium of the Pacific, 100 Aquarium Way, Rainbow Harbor
**Cost:** Included with general Aquarium admission

The Aquarium's annual Pacific Islander Festival fills the first weekend of June with live music, traditional dance, and island cultural programming. It's not a fitness event in the gym sense, but community, culture, and time near the water are core ingredients of wellbeing — and it's one of the most family-friendly weekends on the Downtown Long Beach calendar. Pair it with a walk along the Rainbow Harbor esplanade and you've built a full restorative Saturday.

### Bixby Knolls Farmers Market — Thursdays

**When:** Thursdays, June 4 and June 11, 3:00–7:00 PM (summer hours)
**Where:** Atlantic Ave. & E. 46th St., Bixby Knolls
**Cost:** Free entry

The Thursday market in Bixby Knolls is the north-of-the-405 answer to the beachside markets — stone fruit season is arriving, and the market accepts EBT, WIC, and CalFresh with Market Match for fresh produce. An evening market is also a legitimate wellness habit: a 30-minute walk through the stalls after work beats another hour of screen time, and you come home with vegetables instead of takeout.

### Downtown Long Beach Farmers Market — Fridays

**When:** Fridays, June 5 and June 12, 10:00 AM–2:00 PM
**Where:** 3rd St. between Long Beach Blvd and Pine Ave, Downtown Long Beach
**Cost:** Free entry

If you work in one of the Downtown towers, the Friday market is the easiest wellness win of the week — it sits squarely in the lunch window. Walk over, get sunlight and steps, and stock up for the weekend. Like the Bixby Knolls market, it accepts EBT, WIC, and CalFresh with Market Match.

### Walk on Wednesdays (WOW) — Belmont Shore

**When:** Wednesday, June 10, gather 7:15 AM, walk leaves 7:30 AM sharp
**Where:** Meets in front of Chase Bank on 2nd Street, Belmont Shore
**Cost:** Free

This long-running Belmont Shore walking group does a brisk one-hour, three-mile loop every Wednesday morning, rotating through twelve routes that all pass water — the ocean, Alamitos Bay, the Naples canals, Marine Stadium, or Colorado Lagoon. It's a built-in social circle plus cardio, and you're done before most people have had coffee.

### Tour the Shore — Saturday Mornings, Belmont Shore

**When:** Saturdays, June 6 and June 13, 7:30 AM
**Where:** Meets at 2nd & LaVerne, Belmont Shore
**Cost:** Free

A gentler counterpart to WOW: a two-to-three-mile Saturday loop where you can walk, skate, or wheel — explicitly open to all levels and abilities. Good for anyone returning to movement after injury or a long sedentary stretch who wants company without pace pressure.

### Los Cerritos / Bixby Knolls Neighborhood Cleanup

**When:** Sunday, June 14, 8:00–9:00 AM (second Sunday of each month)
**Where:** Los Cerritos Park
**Cost:** Free

One hour of bending, reaching, walking, and carrying in service of your own neighborhood. Volunteer cleanups are quietly excellent low-intensity movement, and the mental-health research on purpose-driven community activity keeps stacking up. Bring gloves and water.
