Long Beach is often described as a big city that happens to have a beach. That description undersells it. With five miles of coastline, a sheltered bay that doubles as a beginner paddling paradise, the nation's best kitesurfing conditions at Granada Beach, and a $4-a-session outdoor lap pool that rarely has a wait, Long Beach is one of the most water-accessible cities in the country. Summer solstice is the best possible day to start using it.

Here's what's available, by activity, across Long Beach's waterfront neighborhoods.

## Kayaking in Long Beach: Alamitos Bay and the Naples Canals

The most beginner-friendly water experience in Long Beach is a kayak or SUP on **Alamitos Bay**, the calm protected bay between Belmont Shore and Naples Island.

**Kayaks on the Water** (5411 E Ocean Blvd, Bayshore Beach) is the easiest entry point. Kayaks rent from $12 per hour per person; stand-up paddleboards from $25 per hour with basic instruction included if you want it. The LA Times has repeatedly called it one of the best budget-friendly ways to see Long Beach's most photogenic waterways.

From the put-in, you have two classic routes: paddle the perimeter of Naples Island (about 90 minutes at a relaxed pace, past the canals and gondola docks), or strike out toward the moon-jelly cove at the far end of the bay — a slightly longer trip that's worth it when conditions are calm. The bay is protected enough that even first-timers are comfortable within ten minutes.

**Paddle Board Rentals Long Beach** (6272 E PCH) runs a parallel operation with brand-new boards, free waterproof phone case and Bluetooth speaker included, and a Starbucks on-site. The Naples canals circuit is their signature route. Free parking makes it an easy half-day plan.

For research and route planning, the [Perfect Paddles SUP & Kayak Guide to Los Angeles](https://perfectpaddles.com/paddle-boarding-pro/kayaks-on-the-water/) covers Alamitos Bay in detail.

## Windsurfing in Belmont Shore

**Long Beach Windsurf Center** (3850 E Ocean Blvd) has been the go-to for windsurfing on Alamitos Bay for years. They carry a large inventory of boards, sails, and accessories, and run both group and private lessons for beginners and intermediate riders. The bay's consistently light-to-moderate onshore breeze makes it a reliable training ground — enough wind to learn on, not so much that it's intimidating.

Source: [Visit Long Beach — Ocean Sports](https://www.visitlongbeach.com/things-to-do/sports-recreation/water-sports/)

## Kitesurfing at Granada Beach ("Kite Beach")

Long Beach is regularly listed as one of the best kitesurfing spots in the nation. The reason is **Granada Beach**, a stretch south of Belmont Shore where the geography creates near-ideal conditions: consistent onshore wind, a long open-water run, and enough space that riders aren't on top of each other.

**SoCal Kitesurfing** (79 Claremont Place) teaches the full range, from a 90-minute introduction to a five-day "master kiting" course. Instructors get in the water with students and use two-way radio helmets to coach once riders are moving — meaning you can hear corrections in real time rather than waiting until you come back in. Equipment rental is available alongside lessons.

Source: [Visit Long Beach — Ocean Sports](https://www.visitlongbeach.com/things-to-do/sports-recreation/water-sports/)

## Outdoor Lap Swimming at Belmont Plaza

For fitness swimmers, **Belmont Plaza Olympic Pool** (4320 Olympic Plaza) is underrated. It's a temporary outdoor facility — the original was demolished in 2014 after a seismic study; the new [Belmont Beach & Aquatics Center](https://www.longbeach.gov/press-releases/long-beach-city-council-approves-construction-contract-for-belmont-beach--aquatics-center/) is expected to break ground Summer 2026 and complete in Spring 2028 ahead of the LA Olympics. Until then, the temporary pool remains open year-round.

Drop-in adult lap swim: $4. Olympic-length lanes, outdoor sun, and almost never a long wait outside peak hours. For $4, it's hard to find a more complete workout in Long Beach.

Hours and current lane availability: [longbeach.gov/park/aquatics](https://www.longbeach.gov/park/recreation-programs/aquatics/pools/belmont-pool/)

## Waterbikes on Alamitos Bay

**Long Beach Waterbikes** (164 N Marina Dr, Alamitos Bay Landing) is the novelty option that turns out to be a genuinely good workout. Pedal-powered pontoon vessels accommodate small groups, run one-hour tours of the bay, and offer nighttime glow rides on weekends. The paddling motion engages the legs and core in a way that doesn't feel like exercise until the next morning. Departs from Alamitos Bay Landing, where dinner afterward is an easy add.

Source: [Visit Long Beach — Ocean Sports](https://www.visitlongbeach.com/things-to-do/sports-recreation/water-sports/)

## Jet Ski Tours (Bonus: Catalina)

For something farther afield, **Jetski2Catalina** (700 Queensway Dr, Hotel Maya) runs two-hour guided coastal tours from Long Beach and full expeditions to Catalina Island (22 miles across open ocean, with dolphin sightings common). Wetsuits, safety equipment, and fuel are included.

Not a traditional "fitness" option, but 22 miles of open-ocean riding is more of a full-body cardiovascular event than it looks.

## Rent Before You Buy: Sports Basement

If you want to try surfing, bodyboarding, or SUP without committing to gear, **Sports Basement** (2100 N Bellflower Blvd) rents surfboards, bodyboards, stand-up paddleboards, and wetsuits for men, women, and youth, plus roof racks for the drive to the beach. They also run a buy/sell program for used gear, so you can shop discounted "pre-loved" boards and trade in equipment for store credit.

Source: [Visit Long Beach — Ocean Sports](https://www.visitlongbeach.com/things-to-do/sports-recreation/water-sports/)

## Water Fitness and Corporate Wellness

One underused angle on water fitness: it's inherently low-impact, which means it's accessible to employees at a wider range of fitness levels than gym classes or run clubs. A group kayak outing, a SUP session on Alamitos Bay, or a guided waterbike tour scales from the fitness enthusiast to the person who hasn't exercised in a year. If you manage a Long Beach-area team and want to run a wellness activation that actually gets participation, water sports are worth considering alongside the usual options.
