## This Week in Long Beach Wellness

Mid-June in Long Beach is when the marine layer starts burning off earlier, the evenings stretch long enough for after-work movement outdoors, and the city's wellness calendar settles into its full summer rhythm. Between June 11 and June 20 you can practice ocean-view yoga every single day for whatever you can afford, stock up on produce at two of the city's longest-running farmers markets, and close out the week giving thirty minutes back to the sand in Belmont Shore. None of these require a membership, an app, or more than a few dollars — which is exactly the kind of wellness infrastructure that makes Long Beach unusual among Southern California cities.

Here's what's confirmed for the week ahead, neighborhood by neighborhood.

## Featured Events

### Daily Yoga on the Bluff — Alamitos Beach

**When:** Every day, 11:00 AM; weekday evening sessions at 6:00 PM (seasonal, April–October)
**Where:** Bluff Park, corner of E. Ocean Blvd. and Junipero Ave., Alamitos Beach
**Cost:** Donation-based (suggested $15–20, $7 minimum — no one turned away)

Yogalution's outdoor sessions on the bluff overlooking the Pacific are the most reliable wellness fixture in the city: they run seven days a week, year-round, rain rarely stopping play. June means the evening 6:00 PM weekday sessions are active too, so office workers downtown or in the East Village can catch a sunset practice without driving anywhere. Bring a mat and water; everything else is optional, including the donation.

### Long Beach Marina Farmers' Market — Alamitos Bay, near Naples Island

**When:** Sunday, June 14, 9:00 AM – 2:00 PM (every Sunday)
**Where:** 205 N. Marina Dr., Alamitos Bay Marina parking lot
**Cost:** Free entry

Harbor views, fresh seafood, and produce stands make this the most scenic grocery run in the city. It pairs naturally with a walk around Naples Island's canals afterward — roughly two flat, stroller-friendly miles if you circle the island. If your weekend movement routine has gone stale, a market-plus-canal-loop Sunday is an easy reset.

### Bixby Park Certified Farmers' Market — Cherry Avenue

**When:** Tuesday, June 16, 3:00 – 8:00 PM (every Tuesday); also Saturdays 9:00 AM – 2:00 PM
**Where:** Bixby Park, 130 Cherry Ave.
**Cost:** Free entry

The Tuesday evening market at Bixby Park runs late enough to hit after work, and the Saturday morning edition (June 13 and June 20 this window) is one of the better people-watching spots on the bluff. Prepared food, produce, and handmade goods — and the park itself has enough open lawn that plenty of attendees roll out a mat or stretch before shopping.

### 30-Minute Beach Cleanup — Belmont Shore

**When:** Saturday, June 20, start at 9:30 or 10:00 AM; ends 10:30 AM
**Where:** On the sand at Granada Ave., Belmont Shore
**Cost:** Free (bags, gloves, refreshments, and parking provided)

Justin Rudd's monthly third-Saturday cleanup is a Long Beach institution — short enough that there's no excuse, social enough that people come back every month. Volunteering is legitimately good for mental health, and this one adds sunshine, sand, and a community that has been doing this for over two decades. Dogs are welcome at the adjacent Rosie's Dog Beach. Register at JustinRudd.com/cleanup.

### Looking Ahead: International Day of Yoga — June 21

**When:** Sunday, June 21
**Where:** Studios citywide
**Cost:** Varies

Just past this week's window, International Day of Yoga lands on Sunday, June 21. Long Beach studios have historically marked it with special classes and community sessions — West Coast Sweat in years past has hosted a unity-themed practice — so check your neighborhood studio's schedule late this week as listings firm up.

## Why Mid-June Matters for Long Beach Routines

There's a practical reason to anchor a routine now rather than waiting for a January-style reset: June's marine layer keeps mornings cool, which makes outdoor movement easier on the cardiovascular system than the hotter months ahead. For Long Beach residents managing blood pressure, chronic stress, or persistent low back pain, consistent gentle movement — the kind a daily donation-based class makes possible — tends to beat sporadic intensity. If you're working with a real health condition rather than just looking for a workout, that's where [yoga therapy](/yoga-therapy) comes in: a clinically oriented, one-on-one approach delivered by credentialed yoga therapists, distinct from the group classes listed above.
