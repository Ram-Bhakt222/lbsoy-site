## 0 · Brand Spine (revised)

> **Long Beach School of Yoga is a yoga school for the city of Long Beach. We've trained over 100 yoga teachers since 2014, run senior wellness programs, offer yoga therapy, and stay in touch with our graduates wherever they end up teaching.**

**What we are.** A school — a place that teaches teachers, supports a community of graduates, and offers the public yoga that respects bodies, ages, and conditions. Long Beach as a whole, not one neighborhood.

**What we draw from.** Multiple traditions — Hatha, Vinyasa, Restorative, Yin, Ayurveda, Qigong, and the Integral Yoga tradition that Ram Bhakt was originally trained in. The point is the practice, not any single lineage.

**What we are NOT.** A drop-in flow studio. A wellness app. A chain. A 26-pose hot room. A spiritual marketplace.

**Voice.** Warm, plainspoken, unhurried. Says less. Sounds like a teacher who's been doing this for a decade, not a brand. Doesn't bark CTAs. Trusts the reader.

**Vocabulary rules** (from the existing AI SEO doc — keep enforcing):
- *Yoga therapy* / *yoga therapist* on therapeutic + healthcare-adjacent pages.
- *Yoga teacher* / *yoga class* on hotel and general-public pages.
- Lead with conditions, life, and Long Beach. Not poses or chakras.

### Body

> **Yoga therapy is not a yoga class.**
>
> It's a one-on-one or small-group session designed around a specific condition — back, recovery, postpartum, anxiety, blood pressure, sleep. Every pose adapts. Every breath cue is targeted. Your therapist asks before they touch.
>
> At Long Beach School of Yoga, yoga therapy is offered alongside our teacher training, senior wellness, and online courses. It is one part of what the school does, not the whole of it.

### What yoga therapy is useful for

- Back pain — disc issues, sciatica, post-surgical, chronic
- Postpartum and prenatal — pelvic floor, diastasis, return-to-practice
- Anxiety, sleep, blood pressure — pranayama and yoga-nidra-leaning practice
- Cancer recovery and chronic condition support — adjunct to medical care
- Senior bodies — balance, joint protection, fall prevention

### How a session works

1. **Brief intake** — what's going on, what your doctor or PT has said, what you want to be able to do again.
2. **First session** — assessment, gentle introduction, take-home practice.
3. **Follow-ups** — tailored practice, reassessment, gradual progress.
4. **Optional coordination** — shared notes with your physician or PT, with your written permission.

### Long Beach service area paragraph

> Yoga therapy is offered in Long Beach and online to clients across California. We see clients from across the city and surrounding neighborhoods — no specific neighborhood anchor.

## 9 · Frequently Asked Questions (`/faqs`) — broadened beyond yoga therapy

Description:  Frequently asked questions about Long Beach School of Yoga — teacher training, yoga therapy, senior yoga, online courses, our graduates, and how to get in touch.

### About the school

- **What is Long Beach School of Yoga?** A yoga school in Long Beach, founded in 2014 by Ram Bhakt. We train yoga teachers, run senior wellness programs, offer yoga therapy, and maintain a community of certified graduates teaching across Long Beach and beyond.
- **Where are you located?** We serve the whole city of Long Beach. We're not anchored to one neighborhood. In-person sessions are arranged on a per-program basis. Online sessions and courses are available throughout California.
- **Are you affiliated with Cal State Long Beach?** Long Beach School of Yoga began at Cal State Long Beach in 2014 and ran the early teacher training cohorts there. We continue to draw on that lineage but are not currently a CSULB program.
- **How can I contact you?** Email **longbeachschoolofyoga@gmail.com**. We reply within a few days.

### Teacher training

- **When is the next 200-hour teacher training cohort?** Dates are being finalized. Email **longbeachschoolofyoga@gmail.com** with subject *"Teacher training waitlist"* to be notified.
- **What does the program cover?** Hatha, Vinyasa, Restorative, Yin, Ayurvedic, Senior, Teen, Prenatal, and Private yoga, plus the Yoga Sutras, pranayama, meditation, anatomy, ethics, and the business of yoga.
- **Is the program Yoga Alliance registered?** Yes — RYT-200, taught by an E-RYT 500 lead instructor.
- **Who is this program for?** Aspiring yoga teachers, of course — and also nurses, physical therapists, chiropractors, psychologists, social workers, and anyone wanting deeper knowledge of body, breath, and mind.
- **Will the program return to Cal State Long Beach?** Not currently planned. The next cohort venue is being finalized.

### Yoga therapy

- **What's the difference between yoga therapy and a yoga class?** A yoga teacher leads a group class. A yoga therapist works one-on-one or with small groups, has additional clinical training, and adapts every aspect of the practice to a specific person's condition.
- **Is yoga therapy covered by insurance?** Generally no, but HSA/FSA accounts often reimburse — we provide a superbill on request.
- **Do I need to be flexible to start?** No. Yoga therapy is specifically for people who *can't* do a regular yoga class.
- **Can I see a yoga therapist after surgery?** Yes — we coordinate with your physical therapist or surgeon when relevant, with your written permission.

### Senior yoga

- **Do I need experience to start?** No. Senior programs are designed for the 55+ body — gentle, joint-protective, balance-first.
- **Do I have to get on the floor?** Not unless you want to. Chair-accessible options are part of the design.

### Online courses

- **Who can take the online courses?** Open to LBSOY graduates and to teachers from other schools.
- **Do online courses count for Yoga Alliance continuing education?** Yes, our courses are designed to support YACEP continuing education hours.
- **What topics are covered?** Meditation, pranayama, assists and adjustments, anatomy, Ayurveda, mantras, yoga nidra, and more — the topics our 100+ graduates told us they wanted in our 2022 alumni survey.

### Graduates / community

- **I graduated from LBSOY — can I be added to the graduates board?** Yes. Email **longbeachschoolofyoga@gmail.com** with subject *"Graduates board"* and the details on the [Where Our Graduates Teach](/graduates) page.
- **I'm an LBSOY graduate teaching yoga therapy or with a specialty practice. Can I get listed?** Yes — same process. Add your specialty (prenatal, trauma-informed, kids, seniors, etc.) and your booking link if you have one.

## 10 · Voice rules (DO / DON'T)

### DO

- Lead with what the school *is* — a school, plainspoken.
- Use real graduate voices from the survey when illustrating community.
- Name the proof when it's earned: 100+ graduates since 2014, RYS-200 / E-RYT 500 / Registered Yoga Therapist, Olli partnership, the Cal State origin (past tense).
- Acknowledge real bodies and real life — surgery, postpartum, the move to Spain, the back that's not what it was.
- Trust the reader. Say less.

### DON'T

- "Transform your life," "find your bliss," "manifest," "vibe."
- Anchor to one neighborhood (no "Belmont Shore yoga school").
- Imply current Cal State Long Beach affiliation.
- Mention "Featured in Yoga Teacher Magazine."
- Display addresses or phone numbers on the public site.
- Push CTAs aggressively. No "Book Now" buttons. No urgency timers.
- Show pricing until pricing is decided.
- Mix "yoga therapist" and "yoga teacher" interchangeably — use the right word for the page audience.
